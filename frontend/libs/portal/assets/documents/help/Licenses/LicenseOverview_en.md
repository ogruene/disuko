# License Details

## License Chart Status:

- Only licenses with status "License Chart" have been legally reviewed and are generally allowable, but applicable policies such as allow & deny lists have to be considered

## Classifications:

- Overall, the goal is to make legal risks transparent and help product owners deliver their product in a way that is legally compliant with respect to FOSS licensing.
- The classifications differentiate between three levels of caution. They range from "Alarm" to "Warning" and "Information":
  - <i aria-hidden="true" class="mdi-bell mdi v-icon notranslate v-theme--dark v-icon--size-default text-red"></i> **Alarm** indicates that the classification can potentially carry a high risk.
  - <i aria-hidden="true" class="mdi-alert mdi v-icon notranslate v-theme--dark v-icon--size-default text-warning"></i> **Warning** indicates that the classification can potentially carry a risk. The risk may not be as high or as present as with "Alarm " as it may be necessary to determine if the event triggering the classification assignment actually occurs.
  - <i aria-hidden="true" class="mdi-information-outline mdi v-icon notranslate v-theme--dark v-icon--size-default text-textColor"></i> **Information** is simply a reminder that an obligation may need to be met.
  - Remark: On some views just the highest risk level of a license is displayed, whereas a license typically also contains classifications of lower risk levels.

## License Type:

- **Not declared**: not assessed and not defined yet
- **Open Source**: open source software is generally free of charge, with source code licensed under terms such that anyone can inspect, modify, and enhance
- **non-FOSS**: The superset of freeware and proprietary, which typically lack the freedoms provided by open source, like modify and enhance
- **Freeware**: freeware is software, typically proprietary, that is distributed for free, but lacks the freedoms provided by open source, like modify and enhance
- **Proprietary**: commercial software which is typically provided for a fee, without the freedoms provided by open source, like modify and enhance
- **Public Domain**: public domain is a special case, where software is available for free without being licensed
