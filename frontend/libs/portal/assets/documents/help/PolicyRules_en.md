# Policy Rules

## Overview

- This table lists the policy rules (also known as **Allow & Deny Lists**) for the different distribution models ("use cases")
- Policy rules make it easier to identify critical licenses based on the specific distribution model

## Policy Rule Status

- <i class="v-icon notranslate material-icons mdi mdi-minus-circle text-error" style=""></i> The status **Denied** means that such components are generally not to be approved and should be removed or replaced with another component or functionality by the supplier.
- <i class="v-icon notranslate material-icons mdi mdi-minus-circle text-error" style=""></i> Some licenses are even **Forbidden** in _any_ distribution model; these are denied for use in custom developed software without exception.
- <i class="v-icon notranslate material-icons mdi mdi-alert text-warning" style=""></i> The status **Warned** means that the license of a component is on a **Deny List** requiring investigation and a conscious decision.
- <i class="v-icon notranslate material-icons mdi mdi-check-circle text-green" style=""></i> Generally **Allowed** means, that while the license has been reviewed, the actual use of the particular license and whole license families could still be restricted for specific departments and / or specific distribution cases. This status does not refer to the component's technology, maintenance, code quality or cyber security, which need to be assessed separately.
